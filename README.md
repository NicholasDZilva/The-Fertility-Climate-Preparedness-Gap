Core Thesis
Global fertility decline is scientifically documented, measurable, and time-bound
The timeline to catastrophic fertility collapse is shorter than most climate thresholds
Yet fertility receives:
Orders of magnitude less funding
Minimal media coverage
No coordinated preparedness exercises
The disparity correlates with profit alignment, not threat magnitude or urgency
The paper demonstrates that:
Institutional preparedness scales with market creation
Fertility baseline preservation has no profit model
As a result, fertility decline is structurally invisible
Key Contributions
Baseline Fertility Definition
Fertility baseline is defined as the capacity for natural, unassisted reproduction.
Assisted reproductive technologies (e.g., IVF) are explicitly treated as conditional substitutes, not baseline preservation.
Comparative Crisis Analysis
Fertility crisis preparedness is compared against:
Climate crisis infrastructure
Pandemic simulations (e.g. Event 201–style models)
Cyber crisis exercises (e.g. Cyber Polygon analogues)

Appendices
Appendix A: Legal Brief
Baseline Fertility as a Protected Human Right
This section argues that reliance on assisted reproduction as a substitute for natural fertility violates:
The right to found a family
Non-discrimination principles
Intergenerational rights protections
It establishes that states have both negative and positive obligations to preserve the biological and environmental conditions required for natural reproduction.
